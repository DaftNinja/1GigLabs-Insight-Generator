# Air France-KLM Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A sophisticated, elegant, and modern design direction that reflects Air France-KLM's dual heritage and premium positioning. The aesthetic draws inspiration from the clean, editorial layouts of *Monocle* magazine, combined with the refined, human-centric modernism of brands like *Sonos*. This approach visually translates the airline's strategic precision and brand legacy into a compelling and authoritative narrative.

### Content Density

High density. The presentation is a data-rich strategic analysis. Each slide should efficiently present 3-5 key points with supporting data, charts, or detailed text, prioritizing information clarity and depth. The layout should maintain a content-to-whitespace ratio of approximately 80/20 to maximize the delivery of insights.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #1A1A1A (Deep charcoal black - sophisticated, premium foundation)
- Primary color: #FFFFFF (Pure white - clarity, contrast, and elegance)
- Secondary color: #4A4A4A (Medium gray - hierarchy and structure)
- Accent color: #003B6A (Deep Air France blue - brand heritage and trust)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #212121 (Near-black for optimal readability)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #E0E0E0 (Soft white for comfortable reading on dark surfaces)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Oranienbaum (A high-contrast serif with elegant, sharp lines, conveying a sense of heritage and authority - used for major titles and cover pages)
  - Quattrocento Sans (A clean, classic sans-serif, offering excellent readability and a modern, professional tone - used for section headers and chapter titles)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Warm and highly legible, ensuring clarity for dense information and data - primary body text font)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (A classic serif with soft lines, offering an alternative, highly readable option for text blocks - used for quotes and special emphasis)

## 4. Image Style

Employ high-quality, atmospheric photography with a cinematic and slightly desaturated color grade. Focus on abstract architectural details of airports and aircraft, elegant cabin interiors, and sophisticated travel scenarios. Imagery should feel authentic and refined, avoiding generic stock photos. A shallow depth of field and dramatic, natural lighting can enhance the premium and contemplative mood.
