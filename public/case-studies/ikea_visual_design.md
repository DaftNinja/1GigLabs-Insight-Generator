# IKEA Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A design that is functional, democratic, and optimistic, embodying IKEA's mission to create a better everyday life for the many people. The aesthetic draws from the clean, human-centric layouts of Kinfolk magazine and the simple, functionalist principles of Scandinavian design. The core concept is to create a visual language that feels both accessible and inspiring, using a warm, natural palette and clear typography to reflect IKEA's values of simplicity, sustainability, and democratic design.

### Content Density

Medium Density. This presentation balances strategic analysis with IKEA's accessible, lifestyle-oriented brand. Each slide should feature 2-3 key points, often supported by images of products or home solutions. The layout should be clean and uncluttered, with approximately 20-25% white space to reflect IKEA's philosophy of simplicity and functionality.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #FDFBF7 (Warm off-white, creating a soft, natural canvas)
- Primary color: #0057A3 (IKEA blue, representing trust, reliability, and Swedish heritage)
- Secondary color: #4A4A4A (Warm grey, providing sophisticated, neutral contrast)
- Accent color: #F9D26C (Golden yellow, symbolizing optimism, warmth, and Swedish sunshine)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #333333 (Soft black, ensuring comfortable readability)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #FFFFFF (Pure white, for high-contrast text on blue)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Hedvig Letters Sans (A modern, friendly sans-serif that captures IKEA's accessible and functional brand personality)
  - Quattrocento Sans (A classic, elegant choice that provides excellent readability and a timeless, trustworthy feel)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Clean, clear, and highly legible, making complex business information easy to understand)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (A warm, classic serif that adds a touch of craftsmanship and quality to detailed text)

## 4. Image Style

Bright, natural, and lifestyle-focused photography showing real homes and real people. Images should feel authentic and unstaged, featuring IKEA products in beautiful, functional living spaces filled with natural light. Focus on the details of everyday life and the warmth of home. For product shots, use clean, simple compositions on light backgrounds. The overall mood is optimistic, welcoming, and distinctly Scandinavian.
