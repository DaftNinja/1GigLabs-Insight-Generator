# Tesco Strategic Analysis Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

This design embodies a clean, authoritative, and analytical aesthetic. It draws inspiration from the structured clarity of The Economist and the refined precision of Volvo's design language. The core idea is to present complex business data with objective rigor, using a strong grid system and clean typography to create a sense of trustworthiness and strategic intelligence.

### Content Density

High Density: This style is designed for data-rich business reports and strategic analysis. Each slide should efficiently present 3-5 key points or data visualizations, prioritizing clarity and information depth with a content-to-whitespace ratio of approximately 80/20.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #F8F9FA (Light gray-white, creating a clean, neutral canvas)
- Primary color: #0D3B66 (Deep Tesco blue, conveying trust and authority)
- Secondary color: #A7C6D7 (Soft light blue, providing subtle differentiation)
- Accent color: #E85D4A (Warm coral red, highlighting key metrics and calls-to-action)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #212529 (Near-black for optimal readability)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #FFFFFF (Pure white for contrast)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento (Classic serif with strong academic feel, conveys authority and gravitas for main titles)
  - Quattrocento Sans (Clean, elegant sans-serif for subheadings, offering high readability and modern feel)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Primary body text font, offering excellent readability and neutral, professional tone)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (Classic serif body font, provides traditional, book-like reading experience for detailed text)

## 4. Image Style

Imagery should be professional and authentic, favoring high-quality photography of well-lit, modern retail environments, fresh produce, and diverse customer interactions. Avoid generic stock photos. Data visualizations should be clean and minimalist, using the defined color palette to highlight key insights with a style similar to charts in The Economist.
