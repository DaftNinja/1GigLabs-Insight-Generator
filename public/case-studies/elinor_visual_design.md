# Elinor Batteries Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A clean, modern, and nature-inspired design direction that reflects Elinor's Nordic identity and sustainability mission. The aesthetic draws inspiration from the minimalist sophistication of *Kinfolk* magazine and the functional elegance of *Polestar*. This approach visually translates Elinor's commitment to clean energy and innovative battery technology into a compelling and trustworthy narrative.

### Content Density

High density. The presentation is a data-rich strategic analysis. Each slide should efficiently present 3-5 key points with supporting data, charts, or detailed text, prioritizing information clarity and depth. The layout should maintain a content-to-whitespace ratio of approximately 80/20 to maximize the delivery of insights.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #F8F9FA (Soft light gray - clean, airy foundation)
- Primary color: #2D5A3D (Deep forest green - sustainability, Nordic nature)
- Secondary color: #A3B18A (Sage green - harmony, growth)
- Accent color: #E07A5F (Warm terracotta - innovation, energy)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #2A2A2A (Near-black for optimal readability)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #F8F9FA (Soft white for comfortable reading)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Oranienbaum (A high-contrast serif with elegant, sharp lines, conveying a sense of heritage and authority - used for major titles and cover pages)
  - Quattrocento Sans (A clean, classic sans-serif, offering excellent readability and a modern, professional tone - used for section headers and chapter titles)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Warm and highly legible, ensuring clarity for dense information and data - primary body text font)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (A classic serif with soft lines, offering an alternative, highly readable option for text blocks - used for quotes and special emphasis)

## 4. Image Style

Employ high-quality photography with a focus on clean, minimalist aesthetics and natural elements. Use images that showcase Norwegian landscapes, modern industrial architecture with natural light, and close-ups of sustainable materials. The color palette should be muted and earthy, featuring greens, grays, and natural tones. Avoid overly saturated or staged stock photos in favor of authentic, atmospheric shots.
