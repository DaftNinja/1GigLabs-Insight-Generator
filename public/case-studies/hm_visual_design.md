# H&M Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A design that is fashion-forward, accessible, and sustainability-conscious, capturing H&M's dual identity as a global fashion leader and a driver of circular economy. The aesthetic draws from the clean, editorial layouts of Vogue Scandinavia and the modern, conscious branding of companies like Patagonia. The concept is to create a visual experience that feels both trend-setting and responsible, using a vibrant yet refined palette to convey H&M's mission of making sustainable fashion accessible to all.

### Content Density

Medium-High Density. This presentation balances strategic analysis with visual appeal. Each slide should feature 2-4 key points, often accompanied by fashion-forward imagery or data visualizations. The layout should maintain around 15-20% white space to ensure a clean, uncluttered feel that aligns with modern retail aesthetics.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #FAFAFA (Clean, light background allowing fashion imagery to pop)
- Primary color: #E50010 (Bold H&M red, conveying brand identity and energy)
- Secondary color: #2C2C2C (Deep charcoal, providing sophisticated contrast)
- Accent color: #4CAF50 (Vibrant green, representing sustainability and circular fashion)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #212121 (Near-black, ensuring high readability)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #FFFFFF (Pure white, for high-contrast overlays)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Hedvig Letters Sans (A modern, distinctive sans-serif that captures H&M's fashion-forward and accessible brand personality)
  - Quattrocento Sans (Classic and elegant, providing a sophisticated and highly readable alternative for a premium feel)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Clean, clear, and highly legible, perfect for conveying business data and strategic information)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (Classic and warm, its elegant form is ideal for longer text passages and detailed analysis)

## 4. Image Style

High-quality, editorial-style fashion photography that feels aspirational yet accessible. Images should feature diverse models in natural, candid poses, wearing H&M's latest collections in bright, modern environments. Focus on both full looks and detail shots to highlight quality and sustainability. For non-fashion imagery, use clean, modern shots of stores, sustainable materials, or abstract textures that convey the brand's conscious ethos.
