# Ericsson Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A design that is precise, intelligent, and forward-looking, mirroring Ericsson's role as a leader in connectivity and 5G technology. The aesthetic draws from the clean, data-driven layouts of MIT Technology Review and the sophisticated, minimalist tech-ethos of brands like Polestar. The core concept is to visually represent the invisible networks that power the future, using a system of clean lines, structured grids, and a focused color palette to convey intelligence and global scale.

### Content Density

High Density. This presentation is focused on detailed strategic analysis for a highly technical industry. Each slide will contain 3-5 key points, often including data, charts, or technical diagrams, with a content-to-space ratio of around 80/20 to maximize information transfer.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #F8F9FA (Clean, light grey background for optimal readability)
- Primary color: #002B49 (Deep navy blue, conveying trust, depth, and technological authority)
- Secondary color: #4A5C6A (Slate grey, providing sophisticated contrast and structure)
- Accent color: #00C3FF (Vibrant cyan, representing innovation, data, and connectivity)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #1D2129 (Near-black, ensuring maximum legibility)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #FFFFFF (Pure white, for high-contrast overlays)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Chiron Hei HK (A modern, geometric sans-serif that feels precise and technological, perfect for a 5G leader)
  - Quattrocento Sans (A classic and elegant choice, offering high readability and a sophisticated, timeless feel)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Clean, clear, and highly legible, making complex data and technical information easy to digest)
  - Coda (A friendly, rounded font that provides a softer, more human-centric tone for body text and descriptions)

## 4. Image Style

Imagery should be abstract and conceptual, focusing on the visual representation of networks, data flows, and connectivity. Think light trails, digital overlays on cityscapes, and macro shots of microchips and fiber optic cables. The style is clean, high-contrast, and futuristic, avoiding cliché stock photos. When people are shown, they should be interacting with technology in seamless, natural ways, emphasizing a human-centric future.
