1. Executive Summary

GSK plc stands as one of the world's leading pharmaceutical and vaccines companies, with a rich history dating back to 1715 and a portfolio spanning respiratory diseases, oncology, HIV, and innovative vaccines. Following its strategic demerger of the consumer healthcare business (Haleon) in 2022, GSK has sharpened its focus on biopharmaceutical innovation, delivering strong growth momentum across its core therapeutic areas.

Arexvy RSV Vaccine: Market Leadership Achievement

GSK's Arexvy achieved 82.6% efficacy in preventing RSV-LRTD in adults 60+, with 94%+ efficacy in high-risk populations. The vaccine demonstrated 60-65% reduction in RSV-related hospitalizations, establishing GSK as the first-to-market leader in adult RSV protection.

GSK's strategy centers on four core therapeutic areas: respiratory/immunology & inflammation, oncology, HIV, and infectious diseases. The company is targeting over £12 billion in sales from new products and line extensions by 2026, with 14 new medicines and vaccines in development aiming for multi-blockbuster potential by 2031.

2. Company Overview

2.1 Business Model & Segments

GSK operates through two primary segments: Commercial Operations and Total R&D. The company offers specialty medicines across oncology, respiratory/immunology, inflammation, and HIV, alongside a comprehensive vaccine portfolio protecting against shingles, meningitis, RSV, influenza, hepatitis, HPV, and other infectious diseases.

3.1 Revenue & Profitability

GSK delivered solid financial performance in 2024 with total revenue of £31.38 billion, representing 3.46% growth year-over-year. The company maintained strong gross margins of 71.16% and continues to invest significantly in R&D to drive future growth.

3.2 R&D Investment

GSK invested £6.4 billion ($8.6 billion) in research and development in 2024, representing approximately 20.4% of revenue. This substantial investment underscores the company's commitment to innovation and pipeline development, with expectations for even greater R&D spending in 2025.

R&D Strategy & Pipeline Goals

GSK aims to launch 14 new medicines and vaccines with multi-blockbuster potential (defined as >$1B annual sales) by 2031. The pipeline prioritizes best-in-class or first-in-class opportunities across four core therapeutic areas, with 37 programs in Phase 3 or registration as of early 2024.

Therapeutic Focus Areas:

  • Respiratory, Immunology & Inflammation: Severe asthma, COPD, lupus, eosinophil-driven diseases
  • Oncology: Multiple myeloma, ovarian cancer, endometrial cancer
  • HIV: Long-acting prevention and treatment options
  • Infectious Diseases: Vaccines for RSV, meningitis, influenza, and emerging threats

4. Product Portfolio & Pipeline

4.1 Arexvy: RSV Vaccine Breakthrough

Arexvy (Respiratory Syncytial Virus Vaccine, Adjuvanted) represents a landmark achievement in adult vaccine development, becoming the first FDA-approved RSV vaccine for adults 60 years and older. The vaccine has demonstrated exceptional efficacy in preventing RSV-related lower respiratory tract disease (LRTD).

Market Expansion: Arexvy has achieved increasing sales contributions from Europe and international markets, with launches in Japan (Q3 2024), Spain, and Italy (Q1 2025). GSK established its first vaccine distribution center in the Middle East in Abu Dhabi (July 2025), strengthening regional supply chain capabilities.

Safety Profile: Arexvy demonstrated a favorable safety profile with local injection site pain occurring in approximately 26% of recipients, systemic adverse events in 8-10%, and serious adverse events in <1% of cases—comparable to placebo groups.

5. Strategic Growth Initiatives

GSK's growth strategy is built on three pillars: pipeline prioritization, geographical expansion, and strategic partnerships. The company is targeting sales exceeding £40 billion by 2031, driven by innovation in high-margin therapeutic areas.

Strategic Priorities
  • Pipeline Prioritization: Focus on specialty medicines and vaccines with new product launches and expanded indications
  • Geographical Expansion: Growth in emerging markets to diversify revenue streams, with particular focus on Asia-Pacific (4.2% YoY growth)
  • Strategic Partnerships: Collaborations driving technological advancement and value-based healthcare agreements
  • Global Access: Commitment to making medicines available in low- and middle-income countries

Technology & Innovation Investment:

  • AI/ML Integration: Accelerating drug discovery and clinical trial optimization
  • Manufacturing 4.0: Smart facilities with AI-enabled production systems
  • Genetic Target Validation: Proprietary platforms improving drug candidate success rates
  • Digital Health Solutions: AI and data analytics for integrated healthcare solutions

Intellectual Property Management: In April 2025, GSK and Pfizer finalized a global settlement concerning RSV patents, securing a royalty stream for GSK on sales of Abrysvo, demonstrating effective IP strategy execution.

6. Market Position & Outlook

GSK is well-positioned to capitalize on demographic trends driving healthcare demand, with a strong portfolio addressing aging populations, infectious disease prevention, and cancer treatment. The company's first-mover advantage in RSV vaccines and robust oncology pipeline provide multiple growth drivers.

Key Growth Drivers:

  1. Arexvy Market Expansion: Continued rollout in international markets and potential expansion to younger age groups
  2. Oncology Portfolio Growth: Blenrep expansion into earlier lines of therapy and new combination regimens
  3. Respiratory Franchise: Nucala COPD approval and depemokimab launch
  4. Pipeline Execution: 14 multi-blockbuster candidates advancing toward market
  5. Emerging Markets: Geographic expansion in high-growth regions

Risk Factors: Key risks include regulatory delays, competition from other RSV vaccines (Abrysvo, mResvia), pricing pressure in key markets, and potential pipeline setbacks. The company also faces ongoing litigation exposure, including the Zantac cases that impacted 2024 results.

Investment Thesis: GSK offers a compelling combination of established vaccine leadership, innovative pipeline assets, and strong financial returns. The company's focus on specialty medicines and vaccines positions it well for sustainable growth, while its commitment to R&D ensures continued innovation. The successful launch of Arexvy validates GSK's ability to deliver breakthrough medicines addressing significant unmet medical needs.
