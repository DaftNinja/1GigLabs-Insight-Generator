Executive Summary
Skanska is one of the world's leading construction and project development companies, with operations across the Nordics, Europe, and the United States. Founded in 1887, the company has built a strong reputation for sustainable construction and innovative project delivery.

2024 Key Achievements
Skanska achieved a record-high order backlog of SEK 207.9 billion in 2024, with a book-to-build ratio of 123%. The company opened Phase 1 of the Portland International Airport redevelopment, featuring a notable mass timber roof.
Skanska's purpose is "building for a better society", with a commitment to creating innovative and sustainable solutions that transform the built environment for customers and communities.
Business Model & Four Streams
Integrated Value Chain Approach
Skanska operates through four business streams that represent the Group's operating segments, each with distinct operations, risks, and targets.
Construction
Building and civil construction including hospitals, universities, data centers, highways, bridges, and tunnels. Serves public and private clients.
~88% of Group Revenue
Residential Development
Development of attractive residential homes in the Nordics, Poland, and Czech Republic. Focus on sustainable and energy-efficient housing.
~3-4% of Group Revenue
Commercial Property Development
Development of sustainable and energy-efficient commercial workplaces. Strong focus on green building certifications and long-term value.
~6-7% of Group Revenue
Investment Properties
Active investment in and management of high-quality properties, primarily in Sweden. Includes seven properties valued at SEK 8.2B in 2024.
~1% of Group Revenue

Construction Segment Highlights
Construction represents ~99% of revenue but operates at lower margins (2.8%). The segment's operating margin improved to 3.9% in Q2 2025, up from 3.5% in 2024, driven by cost control and project execution excellence.

Climate Leadership Recognition
Skanska has been named a "Climate Leader" in Europe by Financial Times for two consecutive years. The company co-created the AGC Decarbonization and Carbon Reporting Playbook and signed the Exponential Roadmap supporting the 1.5°C Business Playbook.
Technology & Innovation
Digital Transformation in Construction
Skanska leverages cutting-edge technology to enhance project predictability, efficiency, and quality. The company is at the forefront of adopting digital tools and sustainable building methods.
  • Building Information Modeling (BIM): Comprehensive 3D modeling for project visualization and coordination
  • Mass Timber Construction: Pioneering use of sustainable wood materials, exemplified by Portland International Airport's timber roof
  • AI and Robotics: Implementation of automation tools to improve efficiency and safety
  • Green Building Certifications: Deep expertise in LEED, BREEAM, and other sustainability standards
  • Digital Twins: Virtual representations for facility management and operations optimization
    • Innovation Highlights
    Skanska developed the Insight tool for identifying operational carbon reduction opportunities and the EC3 tool for capturing embodied carbon. The company is also a Fitwel Champion and WELL Keystone member, promoting healthy building standards.

    Strategic Focus Areas
    Building for Future Growth
    Skanska is strategically focused on high-growth sectors and innovative project delivery models to ensure long-term value creation and margin stability.
  • Public-Private Partnerships (P3): Strategic shift towards higher-margin P3 projects for infrastructure development
  • Operations & Maintenance (O&M): 56.7% of pipeline through 2030 dedicated to long-term O&M contracts
  • Data Centers & Life Sciences: High-growth sectors with strong demand in the US market
  • Healthcare & Education: Stable, essential infrastructure projects with long-term demand
  • Transportation Infrastructure: Roads, bridges, and mass transit projects benefiting from government investment
    • Market Positioning
    Skanska's selective bidding strategy prioritizes projects where it holds competitive advantages. The company focuses on sustainable building practices and green infrastructure to meet growing demand for ESG-compliant projects.
    Strategic Outlook & Risks
    Navigating Market Dynamics
    Skanska is well-positioned for continued growth but faces several market challenges that require strategic navigation and risk management.
    Key Risks & Challenges
  • Supply Chain Disruptions: Ongoing challenges with electrical switchgear and emergency generators
  • Labor Shortages: Persistent workforce constraints affecting project timelines and costs
  • Market Volatility: Weak Nordic housing market and elevated interest rates
  • Material Costs: Rising construction costs and extended customer decision-making timelines
  • Geopolitical Risks: Tariffs on materials and potential supply chain disruptions
    • Growth Outlook
    Analyst Projections: 3.8% average annual revenue growth over next 3 years | US Market: Continued strong demand for infrastructure and data centers | Europe: Stable civil projects, gradual recovery in building markets
    Strategic Recommendations
    Building for Continued Success
    Based on Skanska's strong market position and strategic capabilities, the following recommendations will support continued growth and value creation:
  • Accelerate P3 Strategy: Continue strategic shift towards Public-Private Partnerships for higher-margin, long-term infrastructure projects
  • Expand O&M Portfolio: Grow Operations & Maintenance contracts to create stable, recurring revenue streams
  • Strengthen US Market Position: Leverage strong US presence in infrastructure and data centers for continued growth
  • Enhance Sustainability Leadership: Use net-zero commitment and green building expertise as competitive differentiators
  • Invest in Technology & Digitalization: Continue BIM, AI, and automation investments for improved project efficiency
  • Diversify Geographically: Balance Nordic exposure with continued expansion in high-growth US and European markets
    • Investment Thesis
    Skanska offers compelling investment opportunity as a leading construction and development company with strong US market presence, record order backlog, and clear commitment to sustainability. The company's integrated business model, financial strength, and strategic focus on high-growth sectors position it well for long-term value creation.
