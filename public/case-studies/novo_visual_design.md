# Novo Nordisk Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

A design that is precise, caring, and scientifically rigorous, reflecting Novo Nordisk's mission to defeat diabetes and other chronic diseases. The aesthetic draws from the clean, human-centric modernism of the Danish design tradition and the authoritative clarity of publications like MIT Technology Review. The core idea is to create a visual language that balances complex pharmaceutical science with a deep sense of human empathy and purpose-driven innovation.

### Content Density

High-Medium Density. This presentation requires a balance between detailed strategic analysis and accessibility. Each slide should feature 2-4 key insights, supported by data or diagrams, with a layout that uses approximately 15-20% white space to maintain clarity and a professional, trustworthy feel.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #FAFBFC (Very light blue-grey, creating a clean, clinical canvas)
- Primary color: #1A3A5C (Deep medical blue, conveying trust, authority, and pharmaceutical precision)
- Secondary color: #567C8D (Soft steel blue, providing sophisticated, professional contrast)
- Accent color: #C8A464 (Warm gold, representing care, innovation, and life sciences)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #2C3E50 (Dark blue-grey, ensuring excellent readability)
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #FFFFFF (Pure white, for high-contrast text on dark elements)

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (A classic, elegant sans-serif that conveys trustworthiness and clarity, ideal for healthcare communication)
  - Oranienbaum (A high-contrast serif that adds a touch of classic, established authority, perfect for impactful titles)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (Clean, highly legible, and warm, ensuring that complex medical and financial information is easily digestible)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (A classic serif with excellent readability, lending a formal, credible tone to detailed strategic analysis)

## 4. Image Style

Imagery should be human-centric and authentic, focusing on real people living full lives despite chronic conditions. The style is bright, hopeful, and clean, with a focus on wellness and vitality. Avoid generic stock photos; instead, use images that tell a story of empowerment and care. For abstract concepts, use clean, minimalist shots of laboratory equipment, medical devices, or abstract representations of molecular structures, maintaining a bright and precise aesthetic.
